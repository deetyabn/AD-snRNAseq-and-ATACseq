# AD-snRNAseq-and-ATACseq
snRNA-seq-Conditioned Generation of snATAC-seq Profiles in Alzheimer's Disease 

Nikitha Thoduguli, Deetya Nagri, Andrew Van Dusen, Yolanda Xie

Understanding the gene regulatory mechanisms driving Alzheimer’s Disease (AD) is a crucial but challenging task as the vast majority of GWAS loci are in non-coding regions. snATAC-seq (measuring chromatin accessibility) can help characterize gene regulation, but such data is sparse, noisy, and expensive to generate, while snRNA-seq data is comparatively plentiful. To address this, we built a diffusion model to generate snATAC-seq tracks conditioned on snRNA-seq data. We trained our model using 10,000 paired multi-omic cells from the ROSMAP (Religious Orders Study/Memory and Aging Project) cohort, which consisted of curated postmortem brain tissue from 111 donors with a 50/50 case-control split. First, by pseudobulking our snATAC-seq at the level of cell type neighborhood, we create a well-resolved training dataset that retains cell type heterogeneity. We then built a standard VAE to create 30-D latent space embeddings of the snRNA-seq (cell-state) data, which serve as guidance cues for the diffusion model to generate new snATAC-seq tracks in a cell-state-aware manner. We evaluated our  model on the chromatin accessibility tracks of unseen cell states. Ultimately, our approach may help uncover the cell-type specific gene regulatory patterns involved in Alzheimer’s disease.
